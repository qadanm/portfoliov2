## What I worked on

Sawa's marketing site and content structure. Not the app itself — that's a separate product team.

The brief was tone alignment: carry the same calm, lightweight feel from the app into the surface that introduces it. The first impression has to match what using Sawa actually feels like, which means the site has to be as low-friction as the thing it's selling.

## The premise

Sawa is a group-decision app for dining. The challenge isn't finding options — there are too many. The challenge is getting people to agree.

The app is built around three things: reducing friction in group decisions, simplifying interaction, keeping flows lightweight. A group-decision product fails the moment it asks people to do work. Every extra tap is a place where someone drops out and the group breaks.

The marketing site has to mirror that.

## Two decisions that shaped the site

**Content as proof, not promises.** A live matches feed shows the product in motion — real squads, real restaurants, real time — instead of saying "trust us". A curated City Food Guides section sources from Reddit threads with attribution kept intact, so the site reads as built around real places, not just a swipe mechanic.

**One-number install path.** No account creation, no profile setup before the user has seen the value. SMS in, app link out. Friction in onboarding is friction in the product itself.

## The shape of the site

Hero, live matches feed, city guides, long-form restaurant articles, install path. Each piece earns SEO surface, builds brand surface, and leads the same reader into the same install prompt at the bottom.

Tailwind across the whole thing, kept close to the underlying component system so the marketing surface stayed cheap to evolve.
